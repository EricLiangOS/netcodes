What is a Hackathon?

Hackathons are social coding events where programmers get together for a short period of time to collaborate and finish a project, regardless of skill level. These projects are quick yet completed, and often seek to tackle a larger issue in the world, and it's never been easier to get started!

Like many other organizations, netcodes seeks to provide fulfilling hackathon experiences to students by giving them the opportunity to compete with their peers for various prizes. Students of any computer science background can join in on our monthly hackathons and collectively brainstorm and implement a project for amazing prizes.

FAQs

Who should participate in netcodes hackathons?

Everyone! Regardless of skill level, hackathons are a great way to learn about coding for students of all levels. Projects will be judged according to both execution and creativity to set an even playing field. Resources are also provided during the hackathons to further aid your project.

What do I need to do to participate?

After completing the registration form, all you need to do is create a project and presentation video and turn it in before the end of the submission period. Our judges will evaluate all the projects and get back to you if you win.

What are projects judged on?

Each project will be judged on the its presentation, functionality, creativity, and relevance to the theme. Each of these categories are weighted equally and scored from 1 to 10, with the highest scoring projects being crowned the winners. The projects can be of any kind (application, website, hardware, etc.) as long as there is a video demonstrating it.

How will prizes work?

Each hackathon's grand prizes for the winners will be announced beforehand, with smaller prizes for the runner up team. These prizes will be based on the sponsors for each hackathon and will either be virtual (giftcards, subscriptions, etc) or mailed to your address.
