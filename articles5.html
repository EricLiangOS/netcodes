Tips on Getting Computer Science Internships

April 13th 2022

By Eric Liang

One great way to learn about new computer science topics and acquire more hands-on experience is through finding internships from tech-related companies. These internships are both a great way to learn and also help in the future with college applications and finding lucrative jobs.

However, it is often difficult for high schoolers without much previous experience to obtain these internships, but it isn’t entirely impossible. As someone who has had internship experience, below are some tips for finding opportunities for internships.

Cold Emailing

The most common approach to getting an internship is to cold email dozens of different institutions in hopes of them accepting you. Since you are contacting the companies or colleges privately, these opportunities are often custom and unique, but also incredibly difficult to get.

When cold emailing these companies or professors for internship opportunities, it’s important to emphasize three things: why you want the internship, why you stand out, and why they should accept you. These reasons and details obviously vary from person to person, but there are some common elements that every email should have.

One major element is having a brief introduction about yourself that lists skills, accomplishments, and other unique bits of information. This not only demonstrates why you are a good candidate, but also makes you, as an applicant, more 2-dimensional and less robotic.

Another important aspect of your email should be a general outline for your goals in the internship. Instead of just asking if there are any internships available, you should specify your goals and how you plan to achieve them. The plan depends on what type of internship you wish to achieve; for instance, you would need to be a lot more specific when detailing a project plan, rather than joining an already existing company. This not only helps solidify your personal goals, but also shows the organization you’re applying for your commitment and vision.

The strategies listed above can also be used for other forms of contacting organizations. One other form of contacting professors and businesses is through LinkedIn, which has the benefit of showing your pre-existing experience, or through other forms of social media. However, it’s important to keep in mind your purpose and uniqueness regardless of your method of contact.

Internship Listings

A more efficient way of trying to get an internship comes in the form of taking advantage of pre-existing opportunities and applying for positions that businesses are looking to fill out. In this case, finding an internship is much more like a job application than blindly reaching out to people, so the process is more streamlined. However, this comes at the cost of there being more competition.

The internship listings obviously vary between companies and time periods, so finding these internship opportunities really depends on staying alert for potential new opportunities. However, there are companies that normally have rolling applications for internships that are year-round, such as Tesla and Google. Thus, the best way to find these opportunities is to continue to search them up.

Throughout the internship application process, the companies usually ask for similar application details, such as a resume, statement of interest, and past experiences.

I won’t go into too much detail on the resume and statement of interest since these are very similar to job applications and there’s a bunch of resources online. However, many students don’t have any past job experiences, so they have to rely on their past club leadership and volunteering. Unless there’s a limit to the number of experiences you can enter in, it’s usually a good idea to list all relevant previous experiences and describe your passion for your position. This helps exemplify your excitement for the internship and how you can maintain similar passion for the internship.

Conclusion

Ultimately, finding a job or college internships is a difficult task for high school students, yet the results are wonderful and well worth the effort. There are a bunch of tips for finding internships, which is very similar to applying for jobs, but the key trait that each successful intern has is a passion for their internship, so be sure to find a position that you’d enjoy participating in.
